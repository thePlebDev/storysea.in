An inspiring journey of an engineering student to 40 lakhs placement!!

by Sourabh Varshney 26 june 2020 3 min read

I'm Sourabh Varshney, Final year Computer Science student. Resident of Ujhani, Budaun, UP. I want to share my journey from my turning point, 11th standard. In the beginning of std. 11, I was not aware about JEE. Once, a senior told me that there exists an exam named JEE through which you can have a seat in reputed engineering colleges like IIT ,NIT, etc. So I wished to prepare for it and shared it with my father. He bought 6 books weighing 10 kg from Delhi. In my school, there was no facility of teaching for JEE. Moreover, they were even unable to complete the board syllabus also. I never lost hope and tried my best to crack JEE. I learnt everything from books myself. My school was 13 km away from my home so I used to complete my homework on the school bus itself when other students used to play games. I managed to score 160 marks in Mains and reserved my seat in the chemical branch in SVNIT.


Still I was not satisfied with my JEE score as well as my branch. I was aware of the branch change option so I decided to score nice CGPA and along with that I was also preparing for JEE as a backup option. Moreover, I was a technically active fresher, won 3 competitions in first year and at night I used to go to Robotics lab for learning. Finally, the first year ended with a lot of memories with my friend circle (the best one I always had). I scored 9.58 CGPA and changed my branch to computer science. Moreover, my JEE score was 220 this year. And this is how I learnt many things from first year. My time management was really tough. Then the second year began. I was part of 4 clubs in college. Drishti, GDG (Google Developer Club), ACM (Association for computer machinery) and Chintan Pratishthan. I started focusing on competitive coding and Machine Learning. I learned a lot from those clubs but I was not able to focus on anything else so I left the clubs in 3rd year and started a new journey.

It was the first day of third year and it was raining like hell. We had our D. E. Shaw coding interview. So, managed to appear in it by walking in about half foot deep water. The questions were pretty straight forward so I solved it in 5 minutes but I was getting segmentation errors in C and was unaware that other programming languages are also allowed. Then, I scored good in aptitude. And complained about the question to Tnp and hackerrank.

I was selected for the interview. The next day, I had to give a test for another company, JPMC, just before the interview. Because of anxiety, I made a mess of that test. Then, my interview was going average when suddenly the interviewer asked about segmentation fault.

Thinking that I don't have a chance, I told them the issue which was in their question. They were surprised as some students solved it. But, I told them the issue was in C language and nobody solved it in C. So, after a few questions, they let me go. After that, the second round went good too. But, due to the average first round, I was sure that I have left chances for 2 companies in a day. Then, swapnil sir came and told me I was selected. After that, the friends took a cruel gpl. Then, this was a year I only enjoyed. In fourth year after PPO, I decided I should improve the college standard. The motivation was from my friends and we took responsibility for it.

I started a mentorship programme for 1st year in which students from 1st year, interested in coding, used to get guidance. This year, I have tried to improve junior's standards in coding in every possible manner. This year, too, was full of night-outs, movies and playing (corridor cricket and cards being the primary). Although things have turned sour due to COVID-19, friendship is the best thing I have found in college and will always remain to be.

To my juniors, I want to say that College is there to enjoy, to make friends for life, to learn (not curriculum, but life skills) but remember stay away from these cigarettes, drugs and alcohol. They will destroy your life as well as your family. Stay away from such seniors who are taking you to such passages.

Saurabh Varshney

He also got admission in IIM INDORE. Now working in DE-SHAW.
